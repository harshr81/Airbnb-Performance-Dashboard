# Airbnb-Performance-Dashboard
📊 Global Airbnb Strategy: Performance & Insights Dashboard An interactive data visualization tool built to explore global rental trends—focusing on market dominance, service quality metrics, and host verification to drive business growth.


## Introduction

This project focuses on real analytical thinking to solve everyday business questions. I explored how to connect different pieces of information (Data Modeling) and used simple formulas (DAX) to uncover interesting stories. The dashboard looks at which cities lead the market, when people travel the most (seasonality), how prices compare to hotels, and what makes a guest feel safe (trust insights).

## Skills Showcased

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

- **💻 Power BI Desktop:** Main data visualization platform used for report creation.
- **⚙️ Power Query:** Data transformation and cleaning layer for reshaping and preparing the data.
- **📊 Core Charts:** Utilized **Column, Bar, Line,** and **Matrix Charts** to show the market share and monthly reviews by city.
- **🧮 DAX (Data Analysis Expressions):** Used for calculated measures and simple logic.
- **📑 Buttons & Bookmarks:** Used **Buttons & Bookmarks** To create a seamless navigation experience between charts.
- **🛠️ Data Modeling:** Relationships established among tables to enable smooth cross-filtering.

## Data Source

The information used in this project was provided by **Maven Analytics**.
- **Airbnb Listings & Reviews:** Airbnb data for over 250k listings in 10 major cities, including information about hosts, pricing, location, and room type, along with over 5 million historical reviews.

## Features / Highlights

- **Business Problem:**
    - While the global short-term rental market generates billions in revenue, managers and stakeholders often lack an intuitive way to compare performance across diverse international cities.
    - Key questions such as: Which major cities dominate the market share? Are guest satisfaction issues tied to specific categories like cleanliness or value? and How does our host verification status impact brand trust? are difficult to answer quickly with raw data.

- **Goal of the Dashboard:**
The objective was to transform this fragmented data into a "Decision-Support Tool." By building a unified view I aimed to help regional managers to compare Year-to-Date performance across 10 global cities so that they move away from guesswork and focus towards data-driven actions—such as prioritizing host verification or launching cleanliness-improvement programs in specific underperforming cities.

- **Walkthrough of Key Visuals:**

  - Key Metrics (Top KPIs): Displays high-level summaries including 279,712 total listings, 10 cities, 182,024 hosts, and over 5.3M reviews to provide immediate context of platform scale.
  - Listing Growth (Line Chart): Tracks the platform life cycle from 2008 to 2020, identifying the "Take-off point" in 2012 and the "Peak point" in 2019 before the pandemic decline.
  - Market Share by City: A line and stacked column chart showing that Paris, New York, and Sydney dominate the platform, accounting for nearly 48% of total reviews.
  - Interactive Ratings Navigation (Custom Buttons): Two interactive buttons allow users to toggle views: a Star Button for high-level overall ratings and a Magnifying Glass Button for a detailed heatmap of categories like Cleanliness and Value.
  - Pricing Comparison (Bar Chart): Compares the average price of Airbnb property types against hotel rooms, showing that "Entire Places" ($673) often provide a competitive alternative to hotels ($800).
  - Review Seasonality (Line Chart): Visualizes monthly review trends, highlighting peak summer travel in Europe (Paris/Rome) versus holiday season increases in New York.
  - Trust Shield: Analyzes host reliability by showing that 66.9% of hosts are fully verified with profile pictures, helping identify the "Trust Gap" among unverified users.

- **Business Impact & Insights:**
  - Marketing & Strategic Growth: Teams can use market share and seasonality data to focus advertising efforts on high-volume cities like Paris and New York during their peak travel months. This allows the business to align promotional budgets with actual demand, ensuring they are reaching travelers at the right time in the most profitable regions.

  - Quality & Trust: Operations teams can use the detailed ratings heatmap to pinpoint exactly where cities are struggling with cleanliness or value, leading to more effective host-education programs. Additionally, identifying the "Trust Gap" among unverified hosts helps the platform prioritize security updates, which builds stronger guest confidence and long-term brand loyalty.


## Conclusion

This project demonstrates how Power BI can turn complex global data into a practical tool for business analysis. It allows users to filter and explore information easily, making it simple to identify exactly where to focus to improve performance and guest satisfaction.
By organizing data into clear, interactive visuals, this dashboard helps simplify decision-making. It proves that when information is easy to navigate, businesses can act with more confidence to grow and better serve their guests.
